Prokudin-Gorskii Image Alignment Project

Sergei Mikhailovich Prokudin-Gorskii (1863-1944) [Сергей Михайлович Прокудин-Горский, to his Russian friends] was a man well ahead of his time. Convinced as early as 1907 that color photography was the wave of the future, he won the Tzar's special permission to travel across the vast Russian Empire and take color photographs of everything he saw, including the only color portrait of Leo Tolstoy. He recorded three exposures of every scene onto a glass plate using red, green, and blue filters. Although his projection plans never materialized, his RGB glass plate negatives survived and were digitized by the Library of Congress.

Overview

The goal of this project is to take the digitized Prokudin-Gorskii glass plate images and, using image processing techniques, automatically produce a color image with minimal visual artifacts. This involves extracting the three color channels, stacking them, and aligning them to form a single RGB image. Automatic Cropping

To improve the visual quality of the final images, I implemented automatic cropping to remove black borders and excess color borders. The images below show three stages: first the aligned image without cropping, then after removing black borders, and finally after removing color borders. This helps eliminate unwanted artifacts and focuses on the main subject of the photograph.
